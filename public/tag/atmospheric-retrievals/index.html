Atmospheric Retrievals

Cloud and haze parameterization in atmospheric retrievals: Insights from Titan's Cassini data and JWST observations of hot Jupiters
The study investigates the properties of four hot Jupiters observed with JWST (NIRISS, NIRSpec, NIRCam, and MIRI) to infer their atmospheric composition. A strong emphasize is done on the parameterization of clouds and hazes, which are shown to be important for properly interpreting these complex atmospheres.
Quentin Changeat, Deborah Bardet, Katy Chubb, Achrene Dyrek, Billy Edwards, Kazumasa Ohno, Olivia Venot

MIRI-LRS Spectrum of a Cold Exoplanet around a White Dwarf: Water, Ammonia, and Methane Measurements
The atmosphere of WD 0806 b, a Jupiter-like planet orbiting a white-dwarf, as observed by the MIRI instrumnet onboard JWST is interpreted using state-of-the-art atmospheric retrievals.
Mael Voyer, Quentin Changeat, Pierre-Olivier Lagage, Pascal Tremblin, Rens Waters, MIRI-GTO Team
